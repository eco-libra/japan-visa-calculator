Frequently Asked Questions
How much does a Japan tourist visa cost? +
The government fee for a Japan tourist visa is ¥3,000 (single entry) or ¥6,000 (multiple entry). If you use a travel agency or visa agent, additional service fees of ¥5,000–¥15,000 may apply. Citizens of 68 countries can enter visa-free for up to 90 days.
How long does Japan visa processing take? +
Standard processing takes 5 business days after submitting all required documents. Express processing is available in 2–3 business days at some consulates. Processing times may vary by consulate and season — peak travel periods (spring/autumn) can take longer.
Can I work in Japan on a tourist visa? +
No. Working on a tourist or short-term stay visa is strictly prohibited and can result in deportation and future entry bans. If you plan to work in Japan, you must apply for the appropriate work visa (Engineer/Specialist in Humanities, etc.) before arriving.
What is the Japan Digital Nomad Visa? +
Japan launched a Digital Nomad Visa in 2024, allowing remote workers to stay for up to 6 months. Requirements include proof of remote employment or freelance income of at least ¥10 million/year (~$68,000 USD), health insurance coverage, and citizenship from one of the eligible countries. Government fee is approximately ¥6,000–¥9,000.
How do I apply for a Japan work visa? +
To apply for a Japan work visa, you typically need a job offer from a Japanese employer who will sponsor your Certificate of Eligibility (COE). The process: (1) employer applies for COE at immigration office in Japan, (2) COE is sent to you, (3) you apply at the Japanese consulate in your country with the COE. Total process takes 1–3 months.
